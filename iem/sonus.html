7Hz Sonus

A versatile hybrid IEM delivering exceptional clarity and technical performance, perfect for audiophiles and gamers on a budget.

Key Features:

  • Hybrid configuration (1DD + 1BA)
  • 11.3mm dynamic driver for deep bass
  • Impedance: 30Ω
  • Sensitivity: 109dB/Vrms (@1kHz)
  • Frequency Response: 10Hz–20kHz
  • Detachable high-purity silver-plated OCC cable (49 wires per core, 4-core flat structure, 3.5mm termination)
  • Medical-grade resin shells with aviation-grade aluminum faceplates
  • Total Harmonic Distortion (THD): <1% (@1kHz)

Why Choose the 7Hz Sonus?

Priced at $59.99, the 7Hz Sonus offers a neutral-ish sound with a touch of warmth, blending a powerful 11.3mm dynamic driver with a balanced armature for precise mids and treble. Its stellar detail retrieval and wide soundstage make it a top pick for music across genres and gaming, rivaling IEMs twice its price. The lightweight, ergonomic design ensures comfort for long sessions, ideal for daily use.

More Details:

The 7Hz Sonus delivers a balanced sound signature with a slight mid-bass boost and extended sub-bass, providing punchy yet controlled lows, as noted in reviews from Head-Fi and Prime Audio. The midrange is transparent and vocal-forward, with vibrant, textured vocals that shine in pop, jazz, and classical tracks. The BA driver ensures crisp, airy treble without harshness, though some note slight sibilance in female vocals at higher frequencies. Its imaging and instrument separation are exceptional for the price, making it a great choice for FPS gaming where positional audio is critical.

The build quality features medical-grade resin shells paired with sleek aluminum faceplates (available in black, red, or grey), offering durability and a premium aesthetic. The flat, silver-plated OCC cable resists tangling but may feel stiff, and the 3.5mm-only termination limits balanced source compatibility. Accessories include seven pairs of silicone ear tips (blue and white, S/M/L/SS), two sets of nozzle filters (petal and dense), and a faux leather pouch, though some users suggest upgrading ear tips for better fit. With low impedance and high sensitivity, the Sonus is easy to drive with smartphones or budget DACs like the FiiO KA13, scaling modestly with higher-end sources.
