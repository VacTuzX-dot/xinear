Why Choose the XENNS Mangird Tea Pro?

Priced at $359, the XENNS Mangird Tea Pro combines dual 8mm dynamic drivers with six balanced armatures for a balanced yet engaging sound, ideal for gaming and music. Its exceptional imaging and instrument separation make it a top pick for FPS games like Black Ops 6, where pinpointing enemy positions feels effortless. More Details:

The Tea Pro’s sound signature features deep, controlled sub-bass with a midbass focus, delivering punchy lows without overpowering mids, as noted in Headfonics and Head-Fi reviews. The Knowles and Sonion BA drivers produce lush, natural mids, perfect for vocals, and smooth, non-fatiguing treble with crisp details, ideal for long gaming or listening sessions. Its soundstage is wide and deep, with precise imaging, allowing gamers to detect subtle audio cues like footsteps or environmental sounds in titles like Battlefield 2042.

Design and build quality shine, with aluminum shells, medical-grade UV resin, and iridescent faceplates that shift colors from blue to purple under light, making it visually striking. The ergonomic shape ensures comfort for hours, with excellent passive noise isolation (>25dB), great for noisy environments. The modular cable, while slightly stiff, supports 3.5mm and 4.4mm plugs for versatility, though some users note its inflexibility. Accessories include three ear tip types (silicone, powdery silicone, foam), a leather case, and a cleaning brush, though tip selection may require aftermarket options for perfect fit. The Tea Pro is easy to drive, pairing well with dongle DACs or smartphones, and scales with high-end sources for enhanced dynamics.
